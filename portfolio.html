More Than a "Black Box"

A common challenge data scientists face is the "black box" mentality. You can learn how to execute k-means neighbors or a simple linear regression but really knowing what a model does and when it is the best model requires critical thinking and robustness checks. My learning path balances plain learning with application, where I am steadily learning the "work flow of a data scientist" . This includes visualizing the data, exploring the data, and moving through levels of fitness checks to get the best possible model.

In short, I am thinking about why certain models work as much as how they work, making myself a data scientist more prepared for real world data analysis.
