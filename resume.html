Prospect Heights Natural Resource Commission , Restoration Intern

With this internship, I was the sole field restorationist for the last few months of the summer. Five different sites, varying in their ecosystem type (river-bank, prairie, woodlands) were places of potential but not current biodiversity. It was mainly a collection of disturbed soils choked out by aggressive, invasive plant species, and suffering from a litany of environmental challenges such as erosion.
This internship was a fantastic opportunity for me to grow as an environmentalist and as an independent, hands-on problem solver. I was able to take my experiences the previous summer and my own ecological studies (I have learned every common species known to the midwest plant ecosystem by botanical name!) to design my own management strategy. This involved:

  • Independently arriving to the location with the commission's own equipment, driving their vehicle.
  • Surveying the work site, identifying plant species present as well as current ecological challenges.
  • Recognizing problems and deciding on a course of action: either mechanical methods (erosion mats, mowing) or chemical (herbicide application).
  • Preparing and executing planned actions.
  • Reporting on daily activites.
