What is Statistics to Me?

Statistics, to me, is the opportunity to learn how to learn . Through statistical thinking and research, I've developed tools for data visualization, model-fitting accurate regressions, and telling a useful story that uncover the latent information within datasets. This skill, to me, is valuable and exciting - everyday I get to learn more about a new form of regression, a new idea in machine learning, or in application I learn some new fact within whatever field I'm researching. The boundlessness of learning is what excites me about Statistics.

Regressional Analysis of Monarch Butterfly Population Decline

Sparked by my passion for restoration, I have used my Advanced Econometric course requirement for a project methodology and literature review to design a statistical analysis project on what ecologist call the "Milkweed Limitation Hypothesis". Monarch Butterflies are currently experiencing a significant population decline and modern research suggests that the Milkweed plant, which is crucial for Monarch larvae development, has also been declining. I take the current literature focused on the cropland and natural areas of the Midwest and begin developing a framework for accessing the Midwestern urban ecosystem's impact on Milkweed trends and potential Monarch population declines.

Skills I've Developed

  • Familiarity Time-Dependent and Cross-Sectional Regressions, and Weighted Regressions
  • Skills in Developing a Comprehensive and Analytical Literature Review, Contrasting Methodologies
  • Skills in Using Ecological and Spatial Data into Sector-Based Regression Models
